# aLGN™

## AI-Assisted Opportunity Evaluation and Decision Workflow

aLGN™ is a product I designed and built to reduce the repetitive work involved in evaluating job opportunities while improving consistency, transparency, and decision quality.

Rather than relying on a language model to make unrestricted decisions, aLGN™ combines structured criteria, deterministic rules, AI-assisted analysis, browser automation, and human review.

---

## The Problem

Evaluating opportunities manually requires repeatedly reviewing:

- job titles
- responsibilities
- seniority
- compensation
- work arrangement
- domain alignment
- required experience
- application priority

This process can be time-consuming and inconsistent, especially when information is fragmented across job boards, emails, and company career sites.

---

## Product Objective

aLGN™ was designed to answer:

> Which opportunities deserve attention, and why?

The system helps transform fragmented job information into a structured decision workflow.

---

## How It Works

1. Ingest opportunity information from job postings and related sources.
2. Extract relevant details such as title, location, seniority, compensation, and requirements.
3. Evaluate the opportunity against configurable criteria.
4. Apply deterministic rules and AI-assisted analysis.
5. Classify or prioritize the opportunity.
6. Preserve human review for final decisions.
7. Generate structured outputs to support tailored execution.

---

## Product Principles

### Structured Before Generative

AI is most useful when operating within a clearly defined workflow, not when asked to make unbounded decisions.

### Human Judgment Remains Central

aLGN™ supports decision-making. It does not replace final human judgment.

### Explainability Matters

Recommendations should be traceable to identifiable criteria and source information.

### Reusable Capabilities Create Leverage

The workflow separates ingestion, evaluation, decision logic, and output generation so each capability can evolve independently.

    <article class="principle-card">
      <h3>Stable Source of Truth</h3>
      <p>
        Employment history, accomplishments, outcomes, skills, constraints,
        and supporting context remain anchored to verified information.
        Tailoring cannot introduce experience that is not supported by the
        underlying evidence.
      </p>
    </article>

    <article class="principle-card">
      <h3>Proportional Emphasis</h3>
      <p>
        The same experience may contain evidence of product ownership,
        product management, product operations, technical delivery, or AI
        enablement. aLGN™ adjusts which dimensions lead and which remain
        supporting evidence based on the opportunity.
      </p>
    </article>

    <article class="principle-card">
      <h3>Appropriate Abstraction</h3>
      <p>
        Work can be described at multiple truthful levels—from configuring a
        workflow, to delivering a platform capability, to improving an
        operational system. aLGN™ selects the highest defensible level that
        accurately communicates the candidate’s contribution.
      </p>
    </article>

    <article class="principle-card">
      <h3>Evidence Over Keywords</h3>
      <p>
        Role alignment is evaluated by matching employer needs to credible
        supporting evidence, not by simply reproducing language from a job
        description.
      </p>
    </article>

    <article class="principle-card">
      <h3>Confidence-Aware Claims</h3>
      <p>
        Outcomes can be distinguished as formally measured, calculated,
        estimated, inferred, or qualitative. This preserves useful evidence
        without presenting directional observations as audited metrics.
      </p>
    </article>

    <article class="principle-card">
      <h3>Human Review and Accountability</h3>
      <p>
        Users review generated framing, validate assumptions, confirm whether
        claims are defensible, and retain responsibility for the final
        application materials and decisions.
      </p>
    </article>
